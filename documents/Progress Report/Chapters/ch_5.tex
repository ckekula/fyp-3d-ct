\chapter{Future Work}
\label{ch:futurework}

There are several improvements that can be made in the future to further develop this research and improve the overall performance of the proposed system.

\section{Further Improvement of the LC-KSVD Model}

More experiments can be carried out to improve the performance of the LC-KSVD model by changing different training parameters such as dictionary size, sparsity constraints, and regularization values. Testing different parameter combinations may help achieve better classification and localization results.

\section{Studying Other Dictionary Learning Techniques}

This study mainly focused on the LC-KSVD approach. In future research, other dictionary learning methods such as K-SVD, D-KSVD, and FDDL can also be tested and compared. This would help identify which method works best for multi-abnormality detection in 3D CT images.

\section{Validation with Radiologists}

Another important future step is to evaluate the model outputs with the support of radiologists. Their medical expertise can help verify whether the detected abnormal regions and predictions are clinically meaningful and accurate. Such evaluations can improve the practical value of the proposed system.

\section{Comparison with Recent Advanced Models}

Future studies can also compare the proposed approach with newer state-of-the-art models developed for medical image classification and localization. This comparison can provide a clearer understanding of the advantages and limitations of the LC-KSVD-based framework.

\section{Improving Explainability and Reliability}

Future work can focus on making the system more explainable and reliable for real clinical use. Providing clearer visual evidence and more stable predictions may help doctors better understand and trust the AI-generated results during diagnosis.
