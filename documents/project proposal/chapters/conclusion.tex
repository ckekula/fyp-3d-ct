\chapter{Conclusion}
This proposal presents a sparse dictionary learning framework for interpretable lung disease diagnosis from 3D chest CT scans.
By using frozen dictionary learning with LC-KSVD, the proposed system addresses fundamental limitations of existing deep
learning methods like computational complexity, data scarcity, and lack of clinical interpretability. Label consistency
ensures a clear separation between normal anatomical features and pathological patterns, which enables transparent diagnostic
reasoning that clinicians can verify and trust.

The integration of volumetric clinical metrics bridges the gap between machine learning predictions and established radiological
practice. Dictionary atoms provide semantically meaningful representations
that map directly to pathological structures which can be visualized. This approach moved beyond post hoc explanations
towards demonstrating the feasibility of inherently interpretable models.

Benchmarking against K-SVD and 3D CNN baselines will validate the effectiveness of our approach for medical
image classification. The use of publicly available datasets (CT-RATE and ReXGroundingCT) ensures reproducibility for
future research extensions.
Ultimately, this work contributes to the development of transparent AI systems that prioritize clinical interpretability alongside
diagnostic performance, offering a viable alternative to deep learning and other black box approaches in high stakes medical applications.
